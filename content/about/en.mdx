There is a paradox in the AI content market. The more that gets published, the less it informs. Most outlets recycle press releases, translate hype into headlines and confuse novelty with relevance.

**Verso** is the editorial publication of **exímIA**. It was born from a simple premise: those who make business decisions need verified research, not another newsletter summarizing what everyone has already read.

## The problem that drives us

The speed of technological change exceeds most professionals' ability to keep up. In the field of AI applied to business, what was impossible yesterday is a commodity today. And the gap between those who understand the implications and those who merely follow headlines widens every week.

Verso exists to be the filter. Not another aggregator — an editorial curation that separates noise from signal and translates data into decisions.

## How it works

Every publication follows a rigorous process. We research primary sources. We verify data. We cross-check perspectives. When we cite a number, it has an origin. When we express a position, it is grounded.

The content covers four territories — AI and strategy, business and entrepreneurship, technology and innovation, and market trends — always through the lens of those who need to decide, not merely understand.

## Who we write for

For the executive who needs to separate real opportunity from technological hype. For the entrepreneur who wants to apply AI as a strategic lever, not as decoration. For the professional who understands that the advantage isn't in the tool — it's in how you direct it.

If you want the comfortable summary of consensus, there are hundreds of options. If you want the perspective that changes how you think about the problem — you're in the right place.

## Why Verso

In typography, *verso* is the left-hand page of a book — the side opposite the *recto*, the page that appears first. The verso is what's behind. The other side. What most people don't see because they stopped at the surface.

We chose this name because it is exactly what Verso sets out to be: **the perspective from the other side**. In a market saturated with recycled consensus and headlines that confuse novelty with relevance, Verso exists to turn the page — literally — and show what lies behind the obvious.

While the *recto* is what everyone reads, the *verso* is where depth lives. The analysis that demands more than a scroll. The position that makes those who prefer the comfort of consensus uncomfortable. The data point that changes how you think about the problem.

Verso is the other side of the story. The side that matters.
